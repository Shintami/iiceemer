About Taipei

Taipei (/taɪˈpeɪ/), officially Taipei City, is situated in the northern part of Taiwan Island. It is not just the nation's political capital but also the epicentre of commerce, history, and art. The vast transit system allows visitors to get to different places and areas rather easily.

Huashan 1914 Creative Park
Scenic spot

Originally a winery, the Huashan 1914 Creative Park is now an important events venue. Many large-scale exhibitions, installations, theater productions and performances now take place here.

Maokong Gondola
Scenic spot

With a total length of 4.03 kilometers, the Maokong Gondola is the first gondola system in Taipei City. It goes up a hill of tea plantations and, on a clear day, allows magnificent views over Taipei.

Presidential Office Building
Scenic spot

The Presidential Office Building originally served as the seat of the colonial government during the Japanese occupation period. At the time of its completion in 1919, it was the tallest building in Taiwan.

Taipei 101
Scenic spot

Standing in the Xinyi District of Taipei, an area known for its financial services and vibrant shopping malls, TAIPEI 101 represents a worldwide precedent for sustainable skyscraper development.

Yangmingshan Hot Springs
Hot springs

Yangmingshan Hot Springs are mild alkaline sulfuric hot springs with a milky color and a temperate about 70 degrees Celsius, which can increase the numbers of white blood cells and soften skin keratinocytes.

Xinbeitou Hot Springs
Hot springs

Beitou is a mountainous region at the northern part of Taipei City, Taiwan. Beitou hot spring has been famous since Japanese colonial times. The area is around with historic monuments and natural scenic spots.

National Chiang Kai-Shek Memorial Hall
Museum

This memorial hall was built in memory of Chiang Kai-shek, the first president of the Republic of China.

National Palace Museum
Museum

The National Palace Museum houses the world's largest collection of priceless Chinese art treasures, one which spans China's nearly 5,000-year history.

National Dr. Sun Yat-sen Memorial Hall
Museum

This memorial hall was established in memory of Dr. Sun Yat-sen, the father of the Republic of China.

Raohe Street Night Market
Night market & old street

The Raohe Street Night Market covers various kinds of booths for general merchandises, accessories and yummy snacks, all at low and affordable prices.

Shilin Night Market
Night market & old street

The Shilin Night Market is one of the well-organized and most popular night markets. The market is famous for various snacks and eatery.

Wufenpu Garment Wholesale
Night market & old street

Wufenpu is Taipei's largest wholesale clothing market. Over a hundred clothing stores are packed into this area selling the latest fashions at very reasonable prices.

Lungshan Temple
Historic site

The Lungshan Temple houses hundred of statues of Buddhist, Taoist, and Confucian deities. The temple mixes traditional Chinese siheyuan ("four-building courtyard") with palace architecture in its design.

The Red House
Historic site

The Red House was constructed in 1908 by a Japanese architect, which was the first government-built public market in Taiwan and also the most well-preserved Class III historic building in the nation.

Xingtian Temple
Historic site

Xingtian Temple is the most-visited temple in northern Taiwan, attracting upwards of 10,000 people a day. The temple advocates in building a harmonic society from the philosophies of Confucian and Buddhist.

General Information

Climate

Spring ends in late June and the weather is warm with average temperatures between 22℃ (64℉) and 32℃ (80℉).

Currency and Bank

New Taiwan dollar (NT$) is the official currency. Banks open from 09:00 to 15:30, Monday through Friday and are closed on weekends and public holidays.

Electricity

Electricity in Taiwan is 110 Volts, alternating at 60 cycles per second. However, the shape of a socket varies between countries, a portable plug adaptor may be necessary. The flat blade attachment plug is generally accepted.

Language

The official language in Taiwan is Mandarin Chinese while English is the ACM ICMR 2021 official language.

Time

Taipei is 8 hours ahead of Greenwich Mean Time (GMT).

Tipping

In restaurants, hotels or other services, tipping is optional and customized per case depending on whether you are pleased with the service. Consumer taxes are included in price on tags.
